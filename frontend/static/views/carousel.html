Info about the insight!

The 2001 Honda Insight body was purchased on Oct 26, 2013 from a local Craigslist post for $275. The Insight's all aluminum body, after being stripped, weighs only ~300lbs and that is a major advantage to achieving high lateral acceleration. The engine is an EG33 flat six found in a junkyard out of a 1994 Subaru SVX. We kept the subframe with it as well. The radiator is from an e30 and we picked up a pusher fan from an LS400 in the junkyard. One of our members got a Subaru 5MT transmission from a Pull-a-Part in Kentucky out of a totaled outback for $109. We bought a front subframe from a 2003 Crown Vic taxi in Pull a Part. The subframe is all aluminum with double wishbone and wide track width. To make the front bodywork, we build a plywood box that we lined with plastic, and filled with two part polyurethane marine foam.

Info about the e28

Our 2018 challenge car is the legendary 80's BMW E28 with the iconic 5.3 liter Chevrolet LS engine and the TH400 automatic transmission. It features a custom front and and rear suspension biased towards the drag challenge, but still build with handling characteristics in mind. The front was adapted to fit an E36 subframe and coilovers requiring custom controll arms to utilize rack and pinion steering as well as provide better geometry for the car. The back contains a solid rear axle with 3 link and coilover suspension

Info about Chevy S10

2021â€™s Wreck Racing challenge vehicle is a 2001 Chevy S10, Xtreme package, purchased for $275 in South Atlanta with a lot of rust, some half completed bondo work, and a very rad flame themed gauge cluster. The suspension is fully designed and fabricated push/pullrod suspension setup. The S10 sports custom fiberglass bodywork molded off of the original front clip of the vehicle.
